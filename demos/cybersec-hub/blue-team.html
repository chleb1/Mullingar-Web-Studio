The Security Operations Centre

What is a SOC?

A Security Operations Centre (SOC) is a centralised team that monitors, detects, investigates, and responds to cybersecurity incidents 24/7. The SOC is the frontline of an organisation's defence — combining people, processes, and technology to protect against threats.

SOC Tiers

  • Tier 1 — Alert Analyst: Monitors dashboards, triages alerts, escalates incidents
  • Tier 2 — Incident Responder: Deep investigation, containment, remediation
  • Tier 3 — Threat Hunter: Proactive hunting for threats that evaded detection
  • Tier 4 — SOC Manager: Oversight, reporting, process improvement

Blue Team Functions

  • Security monitoring and alert triage
  • Threat detection and hunting
  • Incident response and forensics
  • Vulnerability management
  • Security architecture and hardening
  • Threat intelligence consumption
  • Log analysis and SIEM management

Purple Teaming

Purple team exercises combine red and blue team collaboration — the red team attacks while the blue team observes, learns, and improves detection rules in real time. This accelerates security maturity significantly.

Disciplines

Blue Team Specialisations

Blue team is broad — most professionals specialise in one or more of these areas.

📊

SIEM & Log Analysis

Ingesting, correlating, and analysing log data from across the environment. Writing detection rules. SIEM platforms: Splunk, Microsoft Sentinel, Elastic SIEM, IBM QRadar, LogRhythm.

🔍

Threat Hunting

Proactively searching for threats that evaded automated detection. Uses hypothesis-driven approaches based on threat intelligence and MITRE ATT&CK. Tools: Velociraptor, Elastic, YARA.

🚨

Incident Response

Structured process for containing and recovering from security incidents. PICERL: Preparation, Identification, Containment, Eradication, Recovery, Lessons Learned.

🔬

Digital Forensics

Collecting, preserving, and analysing digital evidence from compromised systems. Memory forensics, disk forensics, network forensics. Tools: Autopsy, Volatility, FTK, Wireshark.

🛡

Endpoint Detection & Response

Advanced endpoint monitoring and response. Detects malicious behaviour, not just known malware. Platforms: CrowdStrike Falcon, Microsoft Defender, SentinelOne, Carbon Black.

🧠

Threat Intelligence

Collecting and contextualising data about threat actors, TTPs, and IOCs to proactively defend. Platforms: MISP, OpenCTI, VirusTotal, AlienVault OTX, Recorded Future.

Frameworks

Essential Blue Team Frameworks

🗺

MITRE ATT&CK

The global knowledge base of adversary tactics and techniques based on real-world observations. Used for threat modelling, detection engineering, and measuring security coverage.

attack.mitre.org →
📋

NIST CSF

National Institute of Standards and Technology Cybersecurity Framework. Five core functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover. The reference standard for enterprise security programs.

nist.gov →
🏗

CIS Controls

18 prioritised security controls that provide a prescriptive, prioritised path to improved cyber defence. Practical implementation guide for organisations of all sizes.

cisecurity.org →
📡

Diamond Model

A model for analysing cyber intrusions across four key attributes: Adversary, Capability, Infrastructure, and Victim. Helps structure threat intelligence analysis.

⛓

Cyber Kill Chain

Lockheed Martin's model describing seven phases of a cyberattack: Reconnaissance, Weaponisation, Delivery, Exploitation, Installation, C2, Actions on Objectives. Used to map defensive controls.

🔺

Pyramid of Pain

David Bianco's model showing the relative difficulty of blocking different types of IoCs — Hash values at the bottom (easy to change), TTPs at the top (costly for attackers to change).

Process

Incident Response Lifecycle

1

Preparation

Build IR playbooks, deploy detection tools, train team, establish communication channels, maintain asset inventories and contact lists. Test IR plans regularly with tabletop exercises.

2

Identification

Determine if an incident has occurred. Analyse alerts, logs, and indicators. Classify severity. Determine scope — how many systems are affected? Is data being exfiltrated?

3

Containment

Short-term: isolate affected systems to stop spread. Long-term: patch vulnerabilities, change credentials. Preserve evidence before containment actions. Document all steps.

4

Eradication

Remove the threat completely — malware, backdoors, persistence mechanisms, rogue accounts. Identify root cause. Ensure the adversary has no remaining access.

5

Recovery

Restore systems from clean backups. Validate integrity. Monitor closely for signs of re-compromise. Gradually return to normal operations.

6

Lessons Learned

Post-incident review within 2 weeks. What happened? What worked? What didn't? Update playbooks, improve detection rules, brief stakeholders, remediate systemic weaknesses.
