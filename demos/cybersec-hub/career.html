Entry Level

SOC Analyst — Tier 1

Security Operations Centre

Monitor SIEM dashboards, triage security alerts, escalate true positives, document incidents. The most common entry point into cybersecurity. Often a 24/7 shift-work environment.

Salary Range (US)
$45k – $75k
Key Certs
Security+, CySA+

Landing Your First Security Role

1

Earn Your Baseline Cert

Security+ is the standard. For offensive roles, eJPT or PNPT also work. Have a certification before applying — it signals commitment and baseline knowledge to screeners.

2

Build a Portfolio

Document your home lab, CTF write-ups, projects, and tools on GitHub or a personal blog. Hiring managers look for evidence of initiative and hands-on experience beyond certifications.

3

Don't Skip the Helpdesk

Many successful security professionals started in IT support. 1–2 years of helpdesk/sysadmin experience provides invaluable context. Apply to helpdesk roles with security responsibilities.

4

Apply Broadly and Early

Apply before you feel "ready". Imposter syndrome is real. Apply to 50+ roles. Junior/entry-level roles, apprenticeships, internships, and government roles (often sponsor certs) are all viable paths.

5

Network Actively

80% of jobs aren't advertised publicly. Attend BSides, DEF CON groups, and local ISSA chapters. Connect with recruiters and professionals on LinkedIn. Referrals dramatically improve your odds.

📝 Resume Tips

  • Lead with a skills section — list tools, languages, and certs prominently
  • Quantify everything: "Reduced alert triage time by 40%", not "improved alerting"
  • List CTF wins, HTB ranks, and TryHackMe streaks as experience
  • Include GitHub link prominently — recruiters check it
  • Tailor every application — use keywords from the job description
  • One page for under 5 years experience, two pages maximum
  • ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) scan for keywords — use exact terms from postings

🎯 Interview Prep

  • Prepare the "tell me about yourself" as a 90-second cyber career story
  • Study common interview questions: CIA triad, how firewalls work, explain a pentest
  • Practice technical scenarios: "How would you investigate a phishing alert?"
  • Know the OSI model layers off by heart — asked in almost every interview
  • Research the company's security posture, breaches, and tech stack beforehand
  • Prepare smart questions about the team, tooling, and growth path
